Instructions

Please read the instructions carefully, and tick each point.

This study examines how people describe objects, so that listeners can identify them.

In each trial, you'll see a display of some characters and/or objects, as well as a sentence introducing the characters/objects (see below). Please click on the sentence after you've read it.

The man above the sausage is on Number 3.

After clicking the sentence, a character/object in the red box will change its location (see below).

Your task is to describe this change of location, so listeners, who don't see this change, will understand the new location of the character/object.

You can use pronouns or nouns to describe this change. For example, you could describe the change as "Now he is on Number 4", or "Now the man is on Number 4". Feel free to vary your expressions.

Important: only the characters/objects in a red box will change their locations. Sometimes there will be more than one red box, and that means either character/object in the red box can change his/her/its location.

Please do not think too much about your responses – we are interested in responses that come to mind first.
